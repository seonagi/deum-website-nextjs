Every platform has different rules. Optimize for each or waste effort.

## YouTube: Long-Form King

**Algorithm priorities:** Retention, CTR, session time, watch time

**Strategy:**
- Hook in first 30 seconds
- Mid-roll payoffs
- Clear thumbnails (high contrast)
- SEO titles
- End screens

**Ideal:** 8-15 minutes

## TikTok: Attention Battlefield

**Algorithm priorities:** Completion rate, rewatches, engagement

**Strategy:**
- Hook in 3 seconds (no intro)
- Vertical video
- Trending audio
- Text overlays (watch without sound)
- Loop-friendly endings

**Ideal:** 15-60 seconds (under 30 preferred)

## Instagram Reels: Visual-First

**Algorithm priorities:** Watch time, saves, shares, completion

**Strategy:**
- Visual hooks
- No watermarks
- Original audio
- Story-style pacing
- Save-worthy content

**Ideal:** 15-30 seconds

## LinkedIn: Professional

**Algorithm priorities:** Dwell time, network engagement, relevance

**Strategy:**
- Professional context
- Clear value
- Subtitles (watch muted)
- Thought leadership
- Native uploads

**Ideal:** 1-3 minutes

## Cross-Platform Strategy

**Don't:** Post same content everywhere
**Do:** Adapt core content for each platform

**YouTube video — repurpose as:**
- TikTok: 3 short clips
- Instagram: Best 30-second segment
- LinkedIn: Professional takeaway
- Twitter: Thread + video

## The Bottom Line

One-size-fits-all doesn't work. Optimize for each platform's algorithm and audience.

It's not about being everywhere-it's about being optimized everywhere.
